## Basics

### World Name
Roznia

## World building

- A low fantasy, no magic world "collided" with a world of magic and monsters centuries ago
- Upon the initial collision, the world was swarmed with powerful monsters, but the people of the time rose up and fought them back. What is left of this collision are "mirrors", portals of varying sizes that are one-way portals from that other world.
- The initial collision infused this world with tons of magic - causing wild mutations of the natural world that we're still getting used to

### Mana, monsters, and essences
- Monsters are born of magic — mana is what animates and shapes them.
- Like energy in our world, this mana is conserved. When a monster is defeated, its mana doesn't vanish; it crystallizes into the creature, which is left behind as a **crystallized essence** drop.
- This is why every monster defeated in combat yields an essence item (e.g. "Essence of the Goblin"), in addition to its normal drops. The essence carries some aspect of the creature it came from, which is what makes it useful in crafting/enchanting.
- See [enchanting.md](enchanting.md) for the gameplay loop built on essences.

### Gems, vessels, and binding
- **Gems are inert mineral lattices that can hold mana.** On their own they carry no magic — they're empty vessels. Their natural crystalline structure is what lets crystallized mana (essence) be *bound* into them. The existing gem descriptions already gesture at this ("magical resonance", "crystalline structure", "prized by enchanters").
- **Clarity = capacity.** The clearer and finer the lattice, the more mana it can hold. So a gem's quality and rarity determine how *strong* an enchantment it can carry — not what kind. (This is the lore basis for "gem → magnitude, essence → effect" in [enchanting.md](enchanting.md).)
- **The vessel is shaped before it is charged.** A raw mined gem is a rough lattice; a *cut* gem (and the ring/amulet it's set into) is a refined vessel with greater, more reliable capacity. Shaping the vessel is mundane fine craft — see [jewelcrafting.md](jewelcrafting.md). Binding mana into it is magic — see [enchanting.md](enchanting.md).

## Gameplay binding
- It would be interesting to allow players to "find" valuable or interesting instances. Maybe you can be gathering and you find a mirror, related to the activity or not, that contains some desirable, rewarding content. Maybe a two-way mirror that contains a pocket dimension with valuable resources or an interesting or desirable setup of monsters to fight, with a boss or something.
